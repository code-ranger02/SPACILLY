## 3. Company Overview

### Vision

To become the most trusted digital marketplace in Africa and globally — where every transaction is secure, transparent, and protected by escrow-backed commerce infrastructure.

### Mission

Spacilly Ltd operates **Spacilly** (https://spacilly.com), a multi-vendor e-commerce marketplace that connects verified buyers and sellers through escrow-protected checkout, dispute resolution, and reliable fulfilment tooling. The Company delivers localized payment options (including mobile money and international cards), seller onboarding and KYC, buyer protection, and scalable platform services designed for real commerce at scale.

### Core Values

| Value | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Trust** | Escrow-first payments, verified sellers, and transparent policies. |
| **Security** | Protection of customer data, platform assets, and financial integrity. |
| **Integrity** | Honest communication, ethical conduct, and accountable leadership. |
| **Innovation** | Continuous improvement of product, technology, and user experience. |
| **Accountability** | Clear roles, documented decisions, and measurable performance. |
| **Inclusion** | Accessible commerce across East Africa and international markets. |

### Objectives

1. Operate and grow the Spacilly marketplace platform (web, mobile, and API).
2. Maintain escrow-protected payment flows and financial integrity across all orders.
3. Onboard, verify, and support sellers while protecting buyer interests.
4. Comply with applicable laws in Rwanda and international data protection standards.
5. Build sustainable revenue through platform commission, seller subscriptions, and value-added services.
6. Protect and expand the Company's intellectual property, brand, and technology assets.
7. Establish professional governance, audit trails, and internal controls as the Company scales.

## 5. Company Governance

Spacilly Ltd operates under a defined governance hierarchy ensuring accountability from shareholders through to operational departments.

```
                    SHAREHOLDERS
                         │
              BOARD OF DIRECTORS
                         │
         CHIEF EXECUTIVE OFFICER (CEO)
                         │
            EXECUTIVE MANAGEMENT
                         │
                   DEPARTMENTS
```

### Governance Principles

1. **Shareholders** hold ultimate authority over constitutional matters, share issuance, and major corporate actions.
2. **The Board of Directors** provides strategic oversight, approves major decisions, and appoints/removes the CEO.
3. **The CEO** is responsible for day-to-day leadership and execution of Board-approved strategy.
4. **Executive Management** (CTO, COO, CFO, and functional heads) implements operational plans within delegated authority.
5. **Departments** execute specialised functions under executive direction and Company policies.

No individual may override a duly passed Board resolution except where emergency powers are explicitly delegated.

## 8. Board of Directors

### Responsibilities

The Board of Directors is responsible for:

- Setting strategic direction and approving annual business plans and budgets
- Appointing, evaluating, and (if necessary) removing the CEO
- Approving major contracts, investments, and capital expenditures above delegated thresholds
- Ensuring legal and regulatory compliance
- Protecting shareholder interests and Company assets
- Overseeing financial reporting, audits, and risk management
- Approving share issuances, amendments to governance documents, and winding-up decisions

### Meetings

| Item | Policy |
|------|--------|
| **Regular meetings** | At least **quarterly** |
| **Notice** | Minimum **7 days** written notice (email acceptable), unless waived by all directors |
| **Format** | In person, video conference, or hybrid — minutes must record attendance and resolutions |
| **Extraordinary meetings** | May be called by any two directors or the CEO with Board consent |

### Voting

- Each director present has **one vote**
- Resolutions are passed by **simple majority** unless the Articles or this Framework require a higher threshold
- The Chairperson has a casting vote in the event of a tie (if designated)
- Directors with a conflict of interest must **declare** it and **abstain** from voting on the affected matter

### Quorum

A quorum for Board meetings is **50% of serving directors, rounded up**, with a minimum of **two directors**. No business shall be transacted without quorum except adjournment.

### Conflict Resolution (Board Level)

1. Conflicts among directors shall first be discussed in a dedicated Board session without management present where appropriate.
2. If unresolved, the Board may appoint an **independent advisor** or **mediator** for a binding recommendation on procedural matters.
3. Persistent deadlock on material matters may be escalated to **shareholders** for resolution.

### Removal of Directors

A director may be removed:

- By **shareholder resolution** in accordance with the Articles of Association and Rwanda company law
- Automatically upon **bankruptcy**, **disqualification**, or ** sustained incapacity** (6+ months)
- By Board resolution where grounds include **gross misconduct**, **breach of fiduciary duty**, or **material conflict of interest** — subject to right of response

### Replacement of Directors

Vacancies shall be filled by **shareholder appointment** or **Board co-option** (if permitted by Articles), until confirmed at the next general meeting. The Company shall update statutory registers within the required filing period.

## 12. Financial Management

### Company Bank Account

(See Section 4.) All funds flow through Company accounts. Escrow-held funds and seller payable balances must be reconciled regularly against MongoDB order and ledger records.

### Expense Approval

Expenses must be:

1. **Business-related** and supported by receipts/invoices
2. **Pre-approved** per Section 19 where above threshold
3. **Recorded** in the accounting system within 30 days

Personal expenses are not reimbursable. Prohibited: cash withdrawals without documentation, split invoices, or false categorisation.

### Budget

- **Annual budget** prepared by CFO, approved by Board before fiscal year start
- **Quarterly reforecast** presented to CEO and Board
- Departments may not commit spend exceeding approved budget without Board approval

### Audits

| Type | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| **Internal review** | Quarterly — CFO + CEO |
| **External audit** | Annually (mandatory when required by law, investors, or Board) |
| **Payment reconciliation** | Monthly — Flutterwave, Stripe, PayPal, bank statements vs platform ledger |

Findings must be reported to the Board with corrective action plans.

### Accounting

- Books maintained in accordance with applicable accounting standards
- Revenue recognised per order lifecycle: payment captured → escrow hold → release on delivery confirmation or policy timer
- Platform commission (~5%) and seller subscriptions recorded as Company revenue
- Seller payable balances recorded as liabilities until disbursement

### Revenue Management

- All checkout revenue processed through approved payment gateways under Company merchant accounts
- Refunds and chargebacks handled per dispute policy; CFO notified of abnormal refund rates
- Multi-currency transactions (RWF, USD, EUR, GBP) recorded with exchange rates documented

### Profit Distribution

Profits (after tax, reserves, and reinvestment obligations) may be distributed as dividends only upon **Board recommendation** and **shareholder approval**.

### Dividend Policy

| Principle | Policy |
|-----------|--------|
| **Priority** | Reinvestment in platform growth, security, and compliance before dividends |
| **Declaration** | Board proposes; shareholders resolve |
| **Timing** | As approved — typically annual or interim if cash position permits |
| **Restrictions** | No dividends if insolvent or in breach of banking/payment partner covenants |

## 16. Security Policy

Spacilly operates a marketplace handling payments and personal data — security is a **Board-level priority**.

### Cybersecurity

- Cybersecurity Officer (may be CTO dual-hatted initially) owns security programme
- Annual risk assessment; penetration testing before major releases where feasible
- Dependency vulnerability scanning on CI pipeline
- Production access restricted to authorised DevOps/engineering leads

### Passwords

- Minimum **14 characters**; passphrase encouraged
- Unique passwords for every system; **no reuse** across Company and personal accounts
- Company-approved password manager required for all Personnel with system access

### Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

**Mandatory 2FA** on:

- GitHub, cloud providers (Render, Vercel, MongoDB Atlas, Cloudinary)
- Payment gateways (Flutterwave, Stripe, PayPal)
- Email and admin dashboards
- Domain registrar and DNS

Hardware security keys preferred for production access.

### Backups

- MongoDB: automated daily backups with **30-day** retention minimum
- Critical configuration and secrets inventory (not secret values) backed up securely
- **Restore test** performed at least **quarterly**

### Encryption

- **TLS 1.2+** for all data in transit (HTTPS, API)
- Sensitive data at rest encrypted per provider standards (MongoDB Atlas encryption)
- Secrets stored in environment variables / secret manager — **never committed to Git**
- PII and payment tokens handled only through approved SDKs and PCI-compliant flows

### Access Levels

| Level | Access |
|-------|--------|
| **L1 — General staff** | Internal tools, non-production environments |
| **L2 — Engineering** | Staging, read-only production logs |
| **L3 — DevOps / CTO** | Production deployment, database admin |
| **L4 — CFO** | Financial systems, payment dashboards |
| **L5 — CEO / Board** | Full oversight; production access only when necessary |

Access granted on **least-privilege** basis; revoked **within 24 hours** of role change or exit.

### Incident Response

1. **Detect & contain** — isolate affected systems
2. **Notify** — Cybersecurity Officer → CEO → Board (within 24h for material incidents)
3. **Assess** — scope, data affected, regulatory notification requirements
4. **Remediate** — patch, rotate credentials, restore from backup
5. **Report** — post-incident review within 14 days; update policies as needed

Material breaches affecting user data: notify Data Protection Officer and comply with Section 17.

- Feature branches: `feature/<ticket>-<description>`
- Hotfix branches: `hotfix/<description>`

Direct pushes to `main` are **prohibited**.

### Branching

1. Create branch from `main` (or `develop`)
2. Implement with atomic commits and clear messages
3. Open Pull Request (PR) with description and linked issue/ticket
4. Pass CI checks before review
5. Merge via **squash or merge commit** per team convention — delete branch after merge

### Testing

- Unit tests for critical business logic (payments, escrow, order state)
- Integration tests for API endpoints where feasible
- Manual QA checklist for checkout, seller payout, and admin flows before release
- **No deployment to production** on failing CI for protected branches

### Code Review

- Minimum **one approving review** from a qualified engineer (two for payment/security changes)
- CTO or delegate approves releases touching payment, auth, or escrow modules
- Reviewers verify: correctness, security, performance, and test coverage

### Deployment

| Environment | Purpose | Access |
|-------------|---------|--------|
| **Local** | Development | All engineers |
| **Staging** | Pre-production testing | Engineering |
| **Production** | Live users — Render (API), Vercel (client) | DevOps / CTO approval |

Environment variables managed per `server/env.example`; production secrets never in repository.

### Documentation

- API changes documented in code and internal docs
- Runbooks for deployment, rollback, and incident response
- Architecture diagrams maintained in `/docs` (e.g. system overview, payment flows)

### Release Approval

| Release Type | Approver |
|--------------|----------|
| Standard feature release | Engineering lead + QA sign-off |
| Payment / escrow / auth changes | CTO + CFO notification |
| Major version / breaking API change | CTO + CEO notification; Board informed if material |

Rollback plan required for all production deployments.

## 20. Conflict Resolution

Spacilly promotes resolution at the lowest appropriate level before escalation.

### Stage 1 — Internal Discussion

- Parties attempt good-faith resolution within **10 business days**
- Direct manager facilitates unless manager is involved — then skip to Stage 2

### Stage 2 — Board Mediation

- Unresolved disputes between founders, directors, or executives referred to **Board** (disinterested directors mediate)
- Board may appoint a neutral internal party to recommend solution
- Decision minuted; parties agree to abide by governance outcome for Company matters

### Stage 3 — Independent Mediation

- Shareholder or founder disputes not resolved at Board level may proceed to **independent mediation** in Kigali (or mutually agreed venue)
- Costs shared equally unless mediator determines otherwise
- Mediation confidential; non-binding unless settlement signed

### Stage 4 — Legal Resolution

- If mediation fails, dispute referred to **courts of Rwanda** or arbitration if provided in Shareholders' Agreement
- Company may seek injunctive relief for IP theft, data breach, or unauthorised fund transfer without completing prior stages

## 21. Resignation & Exit

### Founder Exit

1. **Written notice** to Board (minimum **90 days** unless agreed otherwise)
2. Board determines transition plan for CEO/executive roles
3. **Equity treatment** per Shareholders' Agreement (vesting, good leaver / bad leaver)
4. **IP assignment** confirmed; all Company assets returned
5. Public announcement coordinated by CEO/Board

Founders remain bound by confidentiality (Section 22) and non-compete (Section 23) post-exit.

### Employee Exit

- Notice per employment contract and Rwanda labour law
- Exit interview with HR; access revoked on last working day
- Return of devices, badges, and materials
- Final pay and certificate of service per law

### Share Transfer

- Transfers require **Board approval** and compliance with pre-emption rights
- Share register updated; new shareholder bound by Shareholders' Agreement
- No transfer may circumvent non-compete or confidentiality obligations

### Buy-Back

- Company or remaining shareholders may buy back shares per Shareholders' Agreement valuation formula
- Board approves buy-back price and funding source
- Buy-back shares cancelled or held in treasury per Articles

### Asset Return

Departing Personnel must return or securely destroy:

- Laptops, phones, and hardware
- Access tokens, security keys, and badges
- Copies of confidential documents
- GitHub and cloud credentials (access revoked by IT)

Failure to return assets may delay final payments and trigger legal recovery.

## 22. Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)

All Personnel are bound by confidentiality obligations equivalent to this NDA.

### Confidential Information

Includes all non-public information relating to Spacilly Ltd:

- Business plans, financials, cap table, and investor materials
- Unreleased product features and roadmap
- Security architecture, vulnerabilities, and incident details
- Seller and buyer analytics not publicly disclosed

### Trade Secrets

Includes escrow logic, fraud detection rules, commission structures, proprietary algorithms, and partner terms marked confidential.

### Source Code

All Company source code is confidential. No copying to personal repositories, public gists, or external drives without CTO written approval.

### Customer Data

Buyer and seller personal data, order history, payment metadata, and KYC documents are confidential and protected under data protection law. Access only on need-to-know basis; **no export** for personal use.

### Duration

Confidentiality obligations survive termination **indefinitely** for trade secrets and **5 years** for other confidential information (or longer if required by law).

### Remedies

Breach may result in termination, injunctive relief, and damages. Company may require signed standalone NDA for contractors and advisors.

## 23. Non-Compete Policy

### During Employment / Engagement

Personnel shall not:

- Operate, advise, or invest in a **directly competing marketplace** (multi-vendor ecommerce with escrow/payments) without Board consent
- Solicit Spacilly sellers, buyers, or employees for a competing venture
- Use Company time or resources for competing activities

### After Leaving

For **12 months** following exit (executives and founders: **18 months**):

- No employment or founding role with a **direct competitor** in Rwanda and primary markets where Spacilly operates, without Board written waiver
- No solicitation of Spacilly employees or key sellers/buyers with whom the leaver had material contact

Reasonableness: restrictions limited to roles and geography necessary to protect legitimate business interests. If a court finds a restriction excessive, it may be modified to enforceable scope.

Waivers may be granted by Board in writing for specific activities.

## 24. Amendment Procedure

This Framework may be amended as follows:

   - Director powers or removal procedures
   - IP ownership principles
   - Non-compete or NDA duration
4. **Version control** — Document version incremented; effective date recorded; all Personnel notified within **14 days**
5. **Archive** — Prior versions retained for **7 years**

Operational policies (HR handbook, engineering guidelines) may be updated by CEO with Board notification without full Framework amendment, provided they do not conflict with this document.
